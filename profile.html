Openness 73% Conscientiousness 42% Extraversion 52% Agreeableness 58% Neuroticism 71%

A common result between the three of these tests is that I am more open to explore when it comes to ideas, opinions and learning, disregarding traditional methods. Culture, art and creativity seem to come up a lot, further supporting the fact that I thrive in non traditional settings. I’m actually quite surprised with the niche accuracy of the Myers Briggs test. One critique I have with my results is how creative it says I am, as I often feel doubtful of this.

My Myers Briggs results lean more towards the side of introvert, it also points out that I am less competitive but more cooperative and supportive of social harmony. These two traits would be beneficial in a team environment. However, my negative traits, “neuroticism” in the big five personality test, and “turbulent” in the Myers Briggs test, could cause me to struggle with self doubt about my place in a group situation. I know this to be true as I struggle with anxiety and social anxiety. Something I struggle with the most, trying to "mind read" people, and interpreting it as judgement. Knowing people's personality traits is advantagous for me here as it helps me to understand their though process.

When it comes to forming a team, I think it would be good to have a balance of traditional and non traditional types. The traditional type learners and workers would help steady the ship to stay on task, whereas the non traditional types could help bring something new to the work at hand. The “mediator” personality type thrives with people similar to them, so maybe when forming a group it would be good to find other mediator types, although it may be hard as we form only 4% of the population.
